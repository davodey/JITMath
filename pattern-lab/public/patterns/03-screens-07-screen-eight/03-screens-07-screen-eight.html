calculating price per unit

how to: calculating price per unit

Calculating elasticity of demand involves Division of Fractions, Ratios, Subtraction of Fractions, Rates, Percent, the Cartesian Coordinate Grid, and the Midpoint Formula… Here’s an example elasticity problem…

Which is a better buy: 14.5 oz can for $1.99 or 28 oz. can for $3.59?

Convert to unit price equation: $1.99/14.5 and $3.59/28
Divide: $1.99/14.5 = 13.7 cents; $3.59/28 = 12.8 cents
Step 3/4
Compare: 13.7 < 12.8
Compare: 13.7 = 12.8
Compare: 1.37 < 12.8
Compare: 13.7 > 12.8
